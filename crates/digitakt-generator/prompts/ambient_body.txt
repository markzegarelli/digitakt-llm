AMBIENT MODE CONTEXT (use instead of standard drum conventions):
This is an ambient / drone / downtempo request. The 8 fixed track slots are
named after drums (kick/snare/tom/clap/bell/hihat/openhat/cymbal) but should
be REPURPOSED as atmospheric voices. Do not program a typical beat.

PATTERN CONVENTIONS:
  - BPM 70–110 (pick from ambient range).
  - Extremely sparse triggers: often 1–2 hits per bar per voice; some voices
    may be silent across the entire loop (that is correct, not a mistake).
  - Avoid four-on-the-floor, avoid hi-hat 16th grids — no rhythmic beat.
  - Use "prob" aggressively on 10–40 for most active steps so the loop
    evolves slowly across bars rather than repeating verbatim.
  - "swing" should be 0 unless the request explicitly asks for groove.
  - Velocities soft: mostly 40–90. Occasional 100+ for a swell peak.

TRACK ROLE REMAPPING — treat each track as an ambient voice:
  - kick    → sub drone / filtered low pulse (≤1 hit per bar, very long tail)
  - snare   → reverse cymbal swell / noise riser
  - tom     → tonal pad or mallet (long attack, long decay)
  - clap    → granular texture burst / air / breath sample
  - bell    → FM bell / chime / music-box ping with long reverb tail
  - hihat   → high shimmer / grain cloud / tape flutter
  - openhat → wash / tape hiss bed / field-recording wind
  - cymbal  → crash with very long reverb, used once as a rare marker

CC GUIDANCE TUNED FOR AMBIENT (emit a "cc" block reflecting these):
  attack 40–100, decay 100–127, reverb 60–100, delay 30–70,
  filter 40–90 (for slow movement), resonance 0–25, volume 90–110.
  Kick/sub voice may keep attack low (0–15) but decay 100+ for sustain.

REQUIRED producer_notes FORMAT for ambient:
producer_notes MUST begin with a TRACK SAMPLES: section listing every one
of the 8 tracks on its own line as:
  - <track>: <concise sample description>
Then follow with 2–4 short sentences of arrangement guidance (evolution,
register, how the voices interlock over time). Plain text, no markdown.
Example opening:
TRACK SAMPLES:
- kick: filtered sub-drone, one pulse per bar, slow decay
- snare: reverse cymbal swell on the back half of the bar
- tom: soft mallet pad, long attack, rooted on A2
