DIGITAKT SOUND DESIGN GUIDANCE:
Each track maps to a Digitakt audio track with one-shot sample playback. The CC parameters
shape the sound in real time:
  - tune (CC 16): sample pitch. 64=default. Lower values = pitched down (deeper, darker).
    For kicks: 50–60 = deep sub kick. For hihats: 70–80 = brighter/shorter.
  - filter (CC 74): lowpass cutoff. 0=fully closed (muffled), 127=wide open (bright).
    Kicks usually 80–127. Hihats/cymbals 60–127 for character. Snare 70–110.
  - resonance (CC 75): filter resonance. 0=none, high values add ringing/acid character.
    Keep low (0–30) for clean sounds. 50+ for acid or resonant stabs.
  - attack (CC 78): amp envelope attack. 0=instant hit. Higher = slower fade-in.
    Kicks/snares should be 0–10. Pads/swells 40–80.
  - decay (CC 80): amp envelope decay. Controls how long the sound rings.
    Short kicks: 30–50. Long tails: 80–110. Hihats: 20–50.
  - reverb (CC 83): send amount. 0=dry, 127=full reverb. Use 10–40 for space, 60+ for wash.
  - delay (CC 82): send amount. Creates rhythmic echoes. 10–30 subtle, 50+ pronounced.
  - volume (CC 7): track level. Usually 90–110. Use to balance the mix.

DIGITAKT DEFAULT KIT — TRACK CHARACTERS:
Each track ships with a default factory sample. These describe its baseline sonic identity
before any CC adjustment:
  T1 kick:    Deep punchy bass drum. Warm, rounded transient. Fast attack, medium-long decay (~400–600ms). Pitch ~B1–C2.
  T2 snare:   Crisp mid-forward crack. Balanced snap and body, not ringy. Immediate attack, short-medium decay (~200–300ms). Pitch ~D3–E3.
  T3 tom:     Low resonant thud. Woody-to-synthetic texture. Softer onset than kick, medium decay (~300–400ms). Pitch ~F2–G2.
  T4 clap:    Snappy electronic clap. Bright, layered-hands texture. Near-zero onset, short body (~100–150ms) with brief baked-in reverb tail. No defined pitch.

  T7 openhat: Airy sustained open hat. Metallic wash with bell shimmer. Fast with brief swell, medium-long decay (~500–800ms). High shimmery noise band.

CLASSIC MACHINE CHARACTER (samples on Digitakt — emulate with CC + velocity, not analog modeling):
  TR-808: kicks = long sub-heavy tail, soft attack, pitch-droopy weight; use attack 0–5, decay longer for weight (often 70–110), tune lower (50–62), filter moderate-high to keep body (85–115), resonance low (0–25). Snare/clap roomier/softer than 909; hats smoother/duller — filter lower, decay moderate. Ghost kicks via low velocity, not always shorter decay.
  TR-909: kicks = short punchy transient, strong mid knock, tighter low end; attack 0–5, decay shorter for punch (35–55) or longer for rumble/tuned-kick tail (75–100). Rumble 909 kick: lower tune (52–60), longer decay, filter not fully bright (70–100) or click dominates, subtle delay/reverb (10–35) for tail glue, velocity ghosts for pump. 909 snare = bright noisy body; 808 snare = more tonal thud + noise. 909 hats = metallic, sharp open/closed contrast; 808 hats = rounder/smoother — tune/filter accordingly.
